# The Czech Alphabet (Česká abeceda)

The Czech alphabet is based on the Latin script and consists of 42 letters. It uses diacritics to extend the basic Latin set.

## Notes on Sorting

In Czech dictionaries and lists, the alphabet has a specific sorting order:
1.  **Č** follows **C**.
2.  **Ch** is considered a single letter and follows **H**.
3.  **Ř** follows **R**.
4.  **Š** follows **S**.
5.  **Ž** follows **Z**.
6.  Vowels with diacritics (Á, É, Ě, Í, Ó, Ú, Ů, Ý) are usually sorted as their base letters (A, E, I, O, U, Y) unless the words are otherwise identical.
