# Demonstrative Pronouns (Ukazovací zájmena)

The most common demonstrative is **ten, ta, to** (this/that). It is often used like a definite article ("the") in colloquial Czech.

## Declension (Singular)

| Case | Masculine Animate | Masculine Inanimate | Feminine | Neuter |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Nom** | **ten** | **ten** | **ta** | **to** |
| **Gen** | **toho** | **toho** | **té** | **toho** |
| **Dat** | **tomu** | **tomu** | **té** | **tomu** |
| **Acc** | **toho** | **ten** | **tu** | **to** |
| **Loc** | **tom** | **tom** | **té** | **tom** |
| **Instr** | **tím** | **tím** | **tou** | **tím** |

| **Dat** | **těm** | **těm** | **těm** | **těm** |
| **Acc** | **ty** | **ty** | **ty** | **ta** |
| **Loc** | **těch** | **těch** | **těch** | **těch** |

## This vs. That
To distinguish between "this here" and "that there", add suffixes or separate words:

*   **Tento / Tady ten** = This (here)
*   **Tamten / Tamhleten** = That (over there)
