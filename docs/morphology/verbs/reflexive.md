# Reflexive Verbs (Zvratná slovesa)

Reflexive verbs always come with a reflexive pronoun: **se** or **si**.

## SE (Accusative)
Action reflects back to the subject ("oneself").
*   **Mýt se**: To wash oneself. (*Myji se.*)
*   **Učit se**: To learn (lit. "to teach oneself").
*   **Oblékat se**: To dress oneself.

### Reciprocal Meaning
"Each other".
*   *Milují se.* (They love each other.)
*   *Potkali se.* (They met each other.)

## SI (Dative)
Action is done **for** oneself or to one's own body part/possession.
*   **Mýt si ruce**: To wash one's hands. (Not *Mýt se ruce*!)
*   **Koupit si**: To buy (for) oneself. (*Koupím si auto.*)
*   **Myslet si**: To think (to oneself).

## Reflexiva Tantum
Verbs that **only** exist with *se* or *si*. Without it, they have no meaning.
*   **Smát se**: To laugh. (*Smát* doesn't exist.)
*   **Dívat se**: To look/watch.
*   **Bát se**: To be afraid.
*   **Ptát se**: To ask.
